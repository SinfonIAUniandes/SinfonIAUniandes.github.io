About Us

SinfonIA alliance is an initiative from industry and academia to support the application and development of artificial intelligence and robotics in research projects. This alliance is made up of the Bancolombia Artificial Intelligence Competence Center, the Universidad de los Andes, the Santo Tom√°s University and the University of Magdalena.

Currently, SinfonIA alliance is working on social robotics applications, such as human-robot interaction, autonomous navigation, object recognition through artificial vision, among others. These abilities are developed and put to the test under a semi-humanoid robotic platform, such as the Pepper robot from the Japanese firm Softbank Robotics. The three academic institutions have this robot, which was given on loan by the CCIA of Bancolombia.

Our Tools

For the correct functioning of the tasks executed by the robot, we distribute specialized equipment in the following areas

Manipulation

Works on giving sense to pepper manipulators and arms, we develop poses and trayectories that allow Pepper to manipulate and support objects. We use tools as moveit and various planners to plan and execute different trayectories.

Speech

In speech our job is to make the robot a social entity. We believe communication with the robot is a very essential tool that allows the human-robot interaction in the more natural way posible. We use natural language processing and tools such as speech to text google apis or vosk when offline.

Perception

Our goal is to allow Pepper to understand the world it sees. We focus con different IA models that allow Pepper to identify objects thanks to YoloV7, memorize faces thanks to haarcascade encodings and even read through libraries such as Tesseract

Navigation

We want the robot to explore the world. This is the main goal of navigation, allowing the robot to navigate autonomously, detect obstacles and plan routes to different goal destinations. This is possible thanks to the Navigation Stack, a software that allows graph based planning over customed maps

Toolkit

Programming all the previous tools showed up as a very hard task, due to the native nao language of the robot, because of this we developed the Toolkit, a interface that allows connecting to the robot through ROS easily. The main goal of the Toolkit is offering services and topics that the other tools may use to communicate to the robot and send instructions for its operation.

Interface

The objective of this tool is to develop visual tools that allow control and interaction with the robot in a simple way for various types of users.
